About the Studio

Carshalton Brass Studio (or ‘the garage’ as it was known then) was built in early 2020 as all lessons moved online. Located at the bottom of the garden, sound proofed and treated, it was initially an act of kindness to the neighbours as our teaching hours meant there was music to be heard all day every day from the house. The studio is now equipped to produce the finest online audio possible and is spacious enough for group, as well as individual, lessons.

We provide instrument hire options as well as a rehearsal and recording space. Our online ensembles, such as the #TheBigBrassPlayalong , have proven hugely popular withparticipants from all across the UK taking part.

Music theory and brass arranging lessons are taught as well as any brass instrument. Carshalton Brass Studio has self published a book of brass arrangements entitled 'Duets' as well as 'Trios' and have three pieces for full brass band published with Prima Vista Music.

About Cai and Vic

Cai and Vic met whilst studying the trumpet at the both within individual lessons and group settings.

With the sound of trumpet filling the house on a daily basis, they are both deeply passionate about brass playing and aim to excite and motivate their pupils to the same degree. They endeavour to ensure that every pupil fulfils their potential and offer support to those needing a little extra encouragement.

Huge pride is taken in watching pupils grow and improve. Enjoyment and appreciation are taught alongside a great understanding of all technical aspects of the instrument in all of their lessons and they’re often praised for their efforts to make learning a fun environment.

Come and Learn with us!

Carshalton Brass Studio is run by Cai and Victoria Isfryn who met whilst both studying trumpet performance at the Royal Northern College of Music (Bmus) between 2006 and 2010. Victoria followed this with a masters at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland and Cai at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama. They both have over 11 years experience of teaching all brass instruments at all levels and have led workshops for organisations such at the National Youth Orchestra (where we worked with the most talented young musicians in the country) Halle Outreach, LSO Discovery, Live Music Now (for whom they created projects for disadvantaged children), National Youth Brass Band of Wales, “Codi’r Tô” and “The Nucleo Project” (which are both El Sistema programmes aimed at children of a poorer economic background.)

Play Along in School

We’re offering full class brass tuition which allows the young musicians to learn together and encourage each other in a fun and creative environment. Each pupil will be given a brass instrument and over the course of half a term will be taught the basics of playing by a professional brass musician and teacher. The material used will always be contemporary and relevant with the aim being to perform as a whole class to the rest of the school at the end of the course.

Music is a vital part of the cognitive development of any young pupil and we believe that every child should be given the opportunity and have access to learning an instrument. Our six week course will introduce them to basic concepts of music theory as well as the ins and out of how to create a sound on a brass instrument. We believe that we can develop a culture of curiosity about music making and will get the classroom excited about brass!

If this is something that interests you then please get in touch for prices. Price includes hiring of instruments.
